Weathering

Weathering is the slow breakdown of a material and isÂ categorised into 3 sections based on how the material is broken down. These categories include:

  1. Physical Weathering: This type of Weathering is when the material is broken down by a non living substance that is hitting the material. Eg:Wind, Water, Heat.
  2. Chemical Weathering: This type of Weathering is when the material is broken down by a chemical substance that has a reaction with the material. Eg: Rust happens to steel when it is exposed to moisture or oxygen for to long.
  3. Biological Weathering: the last type of Weathering is when the material is broken down by a living organism. Eg: Earth worms breaking down minerals to get nutrience, Or roots cracking roads and paths.
